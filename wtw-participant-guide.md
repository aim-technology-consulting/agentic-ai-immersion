# AI Capability Guide — Participant Reference
### Willis Towers Watson | Agentic AI Immersion | April 2025

Review the 15 capabilities below and identify the **5 use cases** you want to explore in the workshop. Each section describes what the capability is, what problem it solves, and which hands-on use cases are available to you.

---

## The 15 Capabilities

---

### 1. Conversational AI Agent
*The foundation all other capabilities are built on*

An AI agent is a system that holds a conversation, remembers what was said earlier in that conversation, and takes actions — not just answers questions. You give it a role, a set of rules, and access to tools or data, and it handles the rest. Every other capability in this workshop is an extension of this one.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Financial Services Advisor
- Financial Advisor Basics
- Investment Portfolio Management
- Persistent Financial Advisor
- Banking Operations Center
- Loan Application Discussion

---

### 2. Live Calculation & Analysis
*AI that runs the numbers, not just talks about them*

When given access to a code execution environment, an AI agent can write and run calculations on demand in response to a plain-language request. It shows its working and explains the result. No spreadsheet or analyst required for the computation itself.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Loan & Portfolio Calculator
- Financial Analytics Dashboard

---

### 3. Intelligent Search Across Large Datasets
*Finding meaning, not just matching words*

Traditional search finds documents containing the words you typed. This capability finds content that is conceptually relevant — even if it uses entirely different terminology. It works across large collections of documents, records, and structured data.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Banking Products Catalog
- Loan Underwriting & Risk Assessment

---

### 4. Real-Time Market Intelligence
*AI grounded in what is happening today*

AI models are trained on historical data and go stale. This capability connects an agent to live web search so it can incorporate current information — news, regulatory changes, market movements — into its responses at the moment of the question.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Financial Market Research
- Financial Market Research Portal

---

### 5. Document Intelligence
*Upload a document. Start asking questions.*

Upload any document — a report, a policy, a set of records — and immediately query it in plain language. The AI reads and indexes the content, responds with specific references to what it found, and handles follow-up questions. No manual reading or summarising required.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Banking Document Search
- Loan Policy Document Search

---

### 6. Multi-Step Automated Workflows
*Orchestrating a process, not just answering a question*

Multiple specialist AI agents working in a defined sequence, each handing its output to the next. The workflow runs server-side, can be long-running, and is auditable. Complex, multi-stage processes can be partially automated, with humans stepping in only at decision points.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Insurance Claims Processing

---

### 7. AI Connected to Your Own Systems
*Reaching into live platforms during a conversation*

A standardised way for an AI agent to discover and call external tools and APIs at the moment it needs them. Rather than working only with pre-loaded data, the agent can query live systems — your own or third-party — as part of answering a question.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Platform Operations Assistant
- Documentation Research Assistant

---

### 8. Complex Multi-Source Research
*Answering questions that require joining the dots*

AI that autonomously plans how to answer a complex question by breaking it into sub-queries across multiple knowledge sources, executing them, and synthesising a single coherent answer. It reasons across the results rather than returning a list.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Multi-Source Fraud Investigation
- Loan Underwriting & Risk Assessment

---

### 9. An AI That Remembers Across Sessions
*No starting from scratch every conversation*

After a session ends, the agent extracts relevant facts — preferences, context, decisions — and stores them. At the start of the next session, it recalls what is relevant without being prompted. The experience becomes progressively more personalised over time.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Personalized Banking Assistant

---

### 10. Enterprise Controls & Compliance
*AI that fits inside your governance framework*

A framework layer that adds the controls professional services environments require: complete audit logging of every interaction, human approval gates that pause a workflow pending sign-off, the ability to intercept and modify AI outputs before they reach a user, and programmatic enforcement of business rules.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Transaction Compliance Monitoring
- Trade Execution Logging
- Credit Limit Assessment
- Portfolio Rebalancing
- Customer Service Message Filtering
- Market Data Service Recovery
- Transaction Compliance Screening
- Market Data Enrichment
- Transaction Audit Trail
- Compliance-Ready Conversation Audit
- Insurance Claim Processing Continuity
- Credit Limit Review with Approval
- Large Transaction Authorization
- Loan Advisory with Compliance
- Customer Communication Quality

---

### 11. Distributed Session Management
*Conversations that survive at scale*

Infrastructure that persists conversation state across multiple application instances and restarts, so any server can continue any session without the user losing context. This is a reliability and scaling concern rather than a use case in itself — relevant if you are thinking about deploying AI to many concurrent users.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Distributed Customer Session Management

---

### 12. Multi-Agent Research Teams
*Multiple specialist AIs collaborating on a complex task*

An orchestrating AI coordinates a team of specialist agents working in parallel on different aspects of a problem. The orchestrator assigns tasks, tracks progress, and synthesises a final output. A human can review and approve the plan before execution begins.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Investment Research with Compliance
- Investment Research Report Generation

---

### 13. Monitoring & Observability
*Seeing inside the black box*

Every AI request, response, and tool call is logged with timing, cost, and quality signals. This flows into dashboards that can be queried and alerted on. It is what allows AI systems to be operated like production software — with SLAs, cost controls, and quality tracking.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Wealth Management Advisory Monitoring
- Trade Execution Monitoring
- Customer Service Monitoring
- Loan Processing Pipeline Monitoring

---

### 14. Systematic Quality Testing
*Proving your AI does what you think it does*

A managed service that runs your AI against a defined set of test questions and scores responses across dimensions — accuracy, relevance, safety, task completion. Results are tracked over time so regressions are caught when a model or prompt changes.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Loan Advisory Quality Testing
- Banking Assistant Evaluation
- Banking Operations Tool Validation

---

### 15. Adversarial Security Testing
*Finding vulnerabilities before your users do*

A managed service that systematically attempts to get your AI to produce harmful or policy-violating outputs using known attack techniques — encoded inputs, multi-turn manipulation, jailbreak sequences. It reports which attack types succeeded and how severe each vulnerability is.

**Workshop use cases:**
- Banking AI Security Assessment
